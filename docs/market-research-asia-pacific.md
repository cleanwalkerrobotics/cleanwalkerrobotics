## Executive Summary

The Asia-Pacific region represents a combined addressable market exceeding **USD 25 billion** in annual public space cleaning expenditure, with the autonomous cleaning robot segment projected to grow from USD 592 million (2025) to USD 832 million by 2034 globally. The region contributed over 31% of global cleaning robot market share in 2024, valued at approximately USD 1.91 billion.

Key macro-drivers across the region include:
- **Severe labor shortages** (Japan, Singapore, South Korea, Hong Kong)
- **Extreme heat** making manual outdoor labor dangerous (UAE, Saudi Arabia, India)
- **Prestige-driven smart city programs** with dedicated funding (Singapore, UAE, Saudi Arabia, South Korea)
- **Rising labor costs** outpacing productivity gains in cleaning services
- **Government mandates** for environmental sustainability and carbon reduction

CleanWalker's quadrupedal form factor offers distinct advantages over wheeled competitors (Gausium, WeRide, Enway) in navigating uneven terrain, stairs, curbs, and tight pedestrian zones -- differentiators that matter most in dense Asian cities.

**Top recommendation:** Singapore should be the beachhead market, followed by Seoul, Dubai, Tokyo, and Sydney.

### 1. Singapore

**Regulatory Friendliness:** 5/5
**Ease of Entry:** 5/5
**Estimated Market Size (public cleaning):** SGD 1.2-1.5 billion/year (~USD 900M-1.1B)
**Key City:** Singapore (city-state)

#### Government Spending on Street/Public Space Cleaning

Singapore's National Environment Agency (NEA) oversees public cleansing, with road and pavement cleaning carried out by contracted firms. The broader ASEAN cleaning services market was valued at USD 4.28 billion in 2024, with Singapore commanding a disproportionate share due to high labor costs and stringent cleanliness standards.

The NEA operates an Environmental Robotics Program specifically designed to fund autonomous cleaning technology. The agency awarded two consortia projects for Autonomous Environmental Service Vehicles (AESVs) for road cleaning under this program.

Key budget items:
- **National Robotics Programme (NRP):** Received a **SGD 60 million top-up in 2024**, with RoboNexus accelerator offering mentorship and US research trips
- **Research, Innovation and Enterprise 2025 (RIE2025):** Five-year plan with approximately **SGD 25 billion** allocated for AI, robotics, and deep tech
- **Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG):** Up to **80% co-funding** (capped at SGD 350,000/company) for environmental services technology adoption

#### Organization

- **National level:** NEA sets standards, issues cleaning business licenses, and manages procurement
- **Contracted out:** Public road/pavement cleaning outsourced to licensed contractors
- **Enhanced Cleaning Business Licensing Framework** took effect January 1, 2024, raising standards

#### Existing Autonomous/Robotic Cleaning Deployments

Singapore is the most advanced APAC market for autonomous cleaning:
- **WeRide Robosweeper S6 and S1:** Deployed at Marina Coastal Drive and Esplanade since November 2024 -- Singapore's first commercial autonomous sanitation project. Operates nightly (9 PM-3 AM) and daytime on pedestrian paths. Partnered with Chye Thiam Maintenance (CTM).
- **Enway (Germany):** Received LTA approval for autonomous sweepers on public roads. The "Donner" compact sweeper passed approval tests, supported by the National Robotics Program Office's AESV grant.
- **Gausium:** Has a Singapore entity (Gaussian Robotics Singapore) and is listed on Hong Kong's Smart Government Innovation LAB.
- **SoftBank Robotics Whiz:** Deployed commercially in Singapore for indoor vacuum cleaning.

#### Procurement Process

- Government procurement via GeBIZ (Government Electronic Business) portal
- NEA tenders for cleaning contracts are publicly listed
- Innovation pilots can bypass standard procurement through:
  - NRP grants and AESV program
  - Enterprise Singapore (EnterpriseSG) innovation partnerships
  - PSG direct grants to cleaning companies adopting technology

#### Regulatory Environment for Autonomous Robots

Singapore has the **most developed regulatory framework in APAC:**
- Under the **Active Mobility Act 2017**, autonomous robots on public paths require an Exemption Order from the Land Transport Authority (LTA)
- **Road Traffic (Autonomous Motor Vehicles) Rules 2017** govern AV use on public roads on an opt-in authorization basis
- All AVs must undergo **safety assessments at CETRAN** (Centre of Excellence for Testing and Research of AVs at NTU)
- WeRide obtained both **M1 (public road)** and **T1 (public path)** licenses in under six months
- Singapore ranked **#1 globally** on KPMG's Autonomous Vehicles Readiness Index for policy/legislation and consumer acceptance

#### Smart City / Innovation Programs

- **Smart Nation Initiative:** Overarching national digital transformation program
- **RoboNexus Accelerator:** Offers mentorship, workshops, customer introductions
- **NEA Environmental Robotics Program:** Directly funds autonomous cleaning solutions
- **Open Innovation Platform (OIP):** Connects problem owners with solution providers

#### Import Duties and Trade Barriers

- **Zero tariffs on virtually all goods**, including robotics and machinery. Singapore is a free port.
- **US-Singapore Free Trade Agreement (USSFTA)** ensures all US exports enter duty-free
- No local content requirements for technology imports
- No foreign ownership restrictions

#### Cultural Attitudes Toward Robots

Extremely positive. Singapore has consistently ranked highest in consumer acceptance of autonomous vehicles and service robots. The government actively promotes robotics adoption to address labor shortages.

#### Key Entry Points

- **NEA Environmental Services Industry Transformation Map** -- align with their technology roadmap
- **EnterpriseSG** -- for innovation partnerships and grants
- **NRP/RoboNexus** -- accelerator program access
- **Chye Thiam Maintenance (CTM)** -- already partnered with WeRide, potential distributor
- **CETRAN at NTU** -- for testing and certification

#### Recommended Entry Strategy

Partner with a licensed Singapore cleaning contractor (CTM, ISS, Ramky Cleantech) to co-apply for NEA innovation grants. Leverage the PSG 80% co-funding to reduce customer acquisition cost. Obtain LTA T1 and M1 licenses through CETRAN. Target Marina Bay, Orchard Road, and HDB town centers for initial deployments.

Sources: [Smart Nation Singapore](https://www.smartnation.gov.sg/initiatives/smart-city-solutions/), [Singapore LTA Autonomous Vehicles](https://www.lta.gov.sg/content/ltagov/en/industry_innovations/technologies/autonomous_vehicles.html), [WeRide Marina Bay Deployment](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/11/20/2984259/0/en/WeRide-Robosweepers-Enter-Marina-Bay-Marking-Singapore-s-First-Commercial-Autonomous-Sanitation-Project.html), [Enway Singapore Approval](https://highways.today/2021/01/15/enway-autonomous-sweeper-singapore/), [NEA Public Cleanliness](https://www.nea.gov.sg/our-services/public-cleanliness/overview), [Singapore Import Tariffs](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/singapore-import-tariffs)

### 2. Japan

**Regulatory Friendliness:** 4/5
**Ease of Entry:** 3/5
**Estimated Market Size (public cleaning + contract cleaning):** USD 38 billion by 2035 (contract cleaning services alone)
**Key City:** Tokyo

#### Government Spending on Street/Public Space Cleaning

Japan spends extensively on public sanitation. Local government sanitation expenditures reached approximately **6.3 trillion yen (~USD 42 billion) in 2015** (most recent comprehensive figure), with steady increases since. Municipal waste management costs for the ~1,700 municipalities averaged approximately **2 trillion yen/year** in recent reporting periods.

The Japan Contract Cleaning Services Market is anticipated to reach **USD 38.19 billion by 2035**, growing at a CAGR of 6.25%.

Japan's "2024 Problem" -- a projected shortage of **11 million workers by 2040** with nearly 30% of the population over 65 by 2042 -- is the primary driver for cleaning automation.

#### Organization

- **Municipal level:** Each of Japan's ~1,700 municipalities manages its own street cleaning
- **Prefectural coordination:** Prefectures set standards and allocate budgets
- **Heavily contracted:** Mechanical sweepers and cleaning services largely outsourced to private companies
- **Cultural emphasis:** Japan's cultural norm of cleanliness drives both public spending and private investment

#### Existing Autonomous/Robotic Cleaning Deployments

- **FamilyMart:** Deployed autonomous cleaning robots in **300 outlets** (February 2024)
- **SoftBank Robotics Whiz:** Originally launched in Japan (September 2019), widely deployed in commercial settings
- **Cyberdyne CL02:** Indoor cleaning robot adopted by Kanagawa Prefecture's subsidy program for robot installations
- **Gausium products:** Available through Japanese distributors
- Japan's service robot market is projected to reach **400 billion yen (~USD 2.7B) by 2030**, nearly triple 2024 levels

#### Procurement Process

- Municipal procurement follows formal bidding processes managed by each local government
- National programs via METI (Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry) and MLIT (Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism)
- **Robot introduction subsidy programs** at prefectural level (e.g., Kanagawa Prefecture)
- Relationship-driven: requires local partnerships

#### Regulatory Environment for Autonomous Robots

Japan has been progressively opening regulations:
- **April 2023:** Amended Road Traffic Act allows **Level 4 autonomous mobility robots on public sidewalks**
- Robots classified same as **pedestrians/wheelchairs** (speed capped at ~6 km/h)
- **February 2025:** METI released "Future Perspectives on Autonomous Delivery Robots" roadmap
- METI leading establishment of **international ISO standards** for service robot safety
- Japan Post delivery robots already operating in Tokyo

#### Smart City / Innovation Programs

- **Society 5.0:** National framework for technology-driven social transformation
- **METI Robot Industry Office:** Direct funding and regulatory support
- **Super City Initiative:** Designated zones with regulatory sandboxes
- **National Strategic Special Zones:** Relaxed regulations for innovation pilots

#### Import Duties and Trade Barriers

- Japan applies MFN tariffs on industrial goods; robots and machinery generally face **0-3% duty rates**
- **No US-Japan comprehensive FTA** currently (though bilateral trade deals have been struck on specific sectors)
- Complex **certification requirements** (JIS standards, electrical safety)
- **Language barrier** is significant -- all documentation, manuals, and interfaces must be in Japanese
- **Distribution system** is relationship-heavy and favors established players

#### Cultural Attitudes Toward Robots

The most robot-positive culture globally. Japan has a deep cultural affinity for robots, rooted in Shinto animism and reinforced by popular media (Astro Boy, Gundam). Public acceptance of service robots is extremely high.

#### Key Entry Points

- **METI Robot Industry Division**
- **Japan Robot Association (JARA)**
- **JETRO (Japan External Trade Organization)** -- assists foreign companies entering Japan
- **Local system integrators** in Tokyo (e.g., Hitachi, Panasonic ecosystem partners)
- **Japan Cleaning Industry Association**

#### Recommended Entry Strategy

Enter through a Japanese distribution/integration partner (e.g., a trading company like Mitsui, Marubeni, or a robotics integrator). Target Tokyo's Minato, Shibuya, and Shinjuku wards where labor shortages are most acute. Apply for Super City or National Strategic Special Zone pilot designation. Localize all interfaces and documentation into Japanese. Budget 12-18 months for market entry.

Sources: [Japan Service Robot Market](https://www.ccn.com/news/technology/japans-service-robot-market-to-triple-by-2030-as-labor-shortages-deepen/), [Japan Contract Cleaning Market](https://www.sphericalinsights.com/reports/japan-contract-cleaning-services-market), [Japan Autonomous Robot Regulations](https://itbusinesstoday.com/industrial-tech/service-robots-in-japan-how-regulations-and-public-trust-are-shaping-the-future-of-automation/), [METI Delivery Robots](https://www.meti.go.jp/english/press/2025/0226_002.html), [Street Sweepers in Japan](https://schwarze.com/en/street-sweepers-in-japan-a-blend-of-tradition-and-innovation/)

### 3. South Korea

**Regulatory Friendliness:** 4/5
**Ease of Entry:** 3/5
**Estimated Market Size (cleaning services):** USD 8-10 billion/year
**Key City:** Seoul

#### Government Spending on Street/Public Space Cleaning

Seoul Metropolitan Government has committed to massive robotics investment:
- **202.9 billion won (~USD 158 million)** investment to establish "Global Robot City"
- **Fourth Intelligent Robot Basic Plan (2024-2028):** Over **USD 2.24 billion** in public and private investment by 2030
- Goal: Deploy **one million robots** across all industrial and societal sectors by 2030
- Service robot market expected to grow from USD 11.1B (2020) to USD 16.2B (2024) at 10% annual growth

#### Organization

- **Municipal governments** (Seoul, Busan, Incheon, etc.) manage street cleaning
- **Ministry of Environment** sets national cleanliness standards
- **MOTIE (Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy)** drives robot industry policy
- Contract cleaning model similar to Japan

#### Existing Autonomous/Robotic Cleaning Deployments

- **AIRSTAR service robot:** Deployed at Incheon International Airport Terminal 2 (navigation/service)
- **Gausium products:** Available through Korean distributors
- **SoftBank Robotics Whiz:** Deployed in commercial settings in South Korea
- Korea has been piloting cleaning robots in **subway stations, airports, and government buildings**
- Focus has been on indoor cleaning robots; outdoor autonomous cleaning is an emerging opportunity

#### Procurement Process

- Government procurement through KONEPS (Korea ON-line E-Procurement System)
- **Robot industry promotion subsidies** available through MOTIE
- Dedicated **Robot Land (Incheon)** provides testing and demonstration facilities
- Seoul Metropolitan Government has its own innovation procurement track

#### Regulatory Environment for Autonomous Robots

- **Intelligent Robots Development and Distribution Promotion Act (2008):** Comprehensive legal framework
- Master Plans for Intelligent Robots issued every five years (latest: 2024-2028)
- Government committed to **eliminating 51 regulatory obstacles** to robot deployment
- Goal to raise local manufacturing rate of core robot parts from 44% to 80% by 2030
- Regulatory sandbox programs available for innovative technologies

#### Smart City / Innovation Programs

- **K-Smart City Program:** National smart city development framework
- **Sejong and Busan Smart City National Pilot Cities**
- **Seoul Digital Foundation:** Supports technology pilots in Seoul
- **Korean New Deal:** Includes digital transformation funding

#### Import Duties and Trade Barriers

- **KORUS FTA (2012)** eliminated virtually all tariffs on industrial and consumer goods between US and South Korea
- Remaining tariffs on most industrial goods phased out by January 1, 2021
- **Minimal tariff barriers** for US-made robots
- Korean language localization required
- Strong preference for Korean or Korean-partnered companies in government procurement
- Note: Recent (2025) Trump administration tariffs may create reciprocal friction

#### Cultural Attitudes Toward Robots

Very positive. South Korea has one of the highest robot densities globally (industrial robots per worker). Government actively promotes robots as infrastructure. Strong tech-savvy consumer base.

#### Key Entry Points

- **MOTIE Robot Industry Division**
- **Korea Institute of Robot and Convergence (KIRC)**
- **Seoul Digital Foundation**
- **Incheon Robot Land** -- for testing and demonstrations
- **Korean cleaning service companies** (e.g., Coway, Samsung Welstory facilities services)

#### Recommended Entry Strategy

Partner with a Korean conglomerate or robot distributor. Target Seoul's Gangnam, Jongno, and Yeouido districts for pilot. Apply for MOTIE robot deployment subsidies. Leverage KORUS FTA for zero-tariff entry. Participate in Robot Land demonstration programs.

Sources: [Seoul Robot City Investment](https://koreatechdesk.com/seoul-metropolitan-government-unveils-158-million-investment-plan-to-establish-global-robot-city), [South Korea Robotics Industry](https://www.trade.gov/market-intelligence/south-korea-robotics-industry), [Fourth Robot Plan](https://www.businesskorea.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=209659), [KORUS FTA](https://ustr.gov/trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements/korus-fta), [K-Smart City](https://smartcity.go.kr/en/)

### 4. UAE (Dubai/Abu Dhabi)

**Regulatory Friendliness:** 4/5
**Ease of Entry:** 4/5
**Estimated Market Size (municipal cleaning):** USD 2-3 billion/year
**Key City:** Abu Dhabi (then Dubai)

#### Government Spending on Street/Public Space Cleaning

The UAE, particularly Abu Dhabi and Dubai, spend heavily on public cleanliness as a matter of national prestige:

- **Abu Dhabi Department of Municipalities and Transport (DMT):** Deploying **500+ AI-enabled eco-friendly cleaning vehicles** by end of 2025, including automated street sweepers, washers, water tankers, and specialized boats
- **Autonomous Robosweepers** already deployed on Abu Dhabi Corniche (programmed routes, no human intervention)
- **Dubai Municipality** contracts with major cleaning firms (Imdaad, Dulsco, Bee'ah, Averda) for district-by-district service
- All new vehicles equipped with **IoT for real-time tracking**, **360-degree cameras**, and **AI-powered edge scanners** for waste detection

#### Organization

- **Abu Dhabi:** DMT manages cleaning operations with contracted private firms
- **Dubai:** Dubai Municipality contracts out cleaning to major service companies by district
- **Federal level:** UAE Ministry of Climate Change and Environment sets overarching policy
- **Free zones** have their own cleaning management

#### Existing Autonomous/Robotic Cleaning Deployments

- **Autonomous Robosweepers on Abu Dhabi Corniche** -- operational, cleaning pedestrian areas without human intervention
- **Forte Technology** road cleaning robots debuted in Abu Dhabi (2024)
- **AI-powered edge scanners** being fitted to cleaning vehicles for waste detection
- Dubai and Abu Dhabi ranked **4th and 5th globally** on IMD Smart City Index 2025

#### Procurement Process

- Government tenders through procurement portals (Abu Dhabi: ADDED; Dubai: Dubai Municipality portal)
- **Prestige-driven:** UAE governments actively seek cutting-edge technology for visibility
- **Innovation-friendly procurement:** Willingness to sole-source or pilot innovative technologies
- Free zone entities can import equipment with reduced or zero customs duties
- Government entities often operate under **service-level agreements** with gainshare/penalty provisions

#### Regulatory Environment for Autonomous Robots

- **Generally permissive** for innovation pilots
- No comprehensive autonomous robot legislation yet, but rapid development
- **Dubai Autonomous Transportation Strategy:** Aims for 25% of transportation to be autonomous by 2030
- Abu Dhabi has demonstrated willingness to approve autonomous cleaning robots on public paths
- Approvals tend to be project-based rather than systematic

#### Smart City / Innovation Programs

- **Dubai Smart City Strategy / Smart Dubai Office**
- **Abu Dhabi Smart City Summit** (annual)
- **Dubai Future Foundation / Museum of the Future** -- innovation showcase
- **Mohammed bin Rashid Innovation Fund**
- **Hub71 (Abu Dhabi)** -- technology ecosystem and accelerator

#### Import Duties and Trade Barriers

- **Standard customs duty: 5% CIF value** across the GCC
- **Free zone operations:** Machinery and equipment often **duty-free** in free zones (JAFZA, ADGM, etc.)
- No specific US-UAE FTA, but the 5% rate is low
- **No foreign ownership restrictions** in free zones
- **No income or corporate tax** in free zones (UAE mainland has 9% corporate tax since 2023)

#### Cultural Attitudes Toward Robots

Very positive, especially among government entities. The UAE embraces technology as a symbol of modernity and progress. Autonomous robots in public spaces are seen as aspirational.

#### Key Entry Points

- **Abu Dhabi DMT** -- already deploying autonomous sweepers
- **Dubai Municipality Innovation Department**
- **Hub71 Accelerator** (Abu Dhabi)
- **GITEX / GISEC events** -- major technology exhibitions
- **Cleaning contractors:** Imdaad, Dulsco, Bee'ah

#### Recommended Entry Strategy

Engage directly with Abu Dhabi DMT, which is actively procuring autonomous cleaning technology. Set up a free zone entity (ADGM or JAFZA) for duty-free equipment import. Target the Abu Dhabi Corniche and Dubai Marina/JBR as pilot locations. Emphasize heat-resistance capabilities (a major differentiator for outdoor robots in 40-50C temperatures). Partner with Bee'ah or Imdaad for local distribution.

### 5. Saudi Arabia

**Regulatory Friendliness:** 3/5
**Ease of Entry:** 3/5
**Estimated Market Size (waste management):** USD 14-16 billion/year (total waste sector)
**Key City:** Riyadh

#### Government Spending on Street/Public Space Cleaning

Saudi Arabia is investing massively in environmental services under Vision 2030:

- **SAR 55 billion (~USD 14.7B)** in environmental/waste management initiatives announced in early 2024
- **NEOM and Samsung C&T:** SAR 1.3 billion robotics joint venture aimed at cutting material waste by 40%
- Riyadh Province holds **40.43%** of Saudi waste management market revenue
- Plan to recycle **100 million tonnes of waste annually** and contribute **SR 120 billion (~USD 32B)** to GDP
- Municipal tenders increasingly require integration of **optical sorters, AI robotics, and automated systems**

#### Organization

- **Ministry of Environment, Water and Agriculture (MEWA):** National policy
- **Royal Commission for Riyadh City:** Manages Riyadh's urban services
- **NEOM:** Independent mega-project with its own procurement and governance
- **Municipal governments:** Contract cleaning services
- **Saudi Investment Recycling Company (SIRC):** Government-owned waste management company

#### Existing Autonomous/Robotic Cleaning Deployments

- **NEOM:** Deployed NOMADD autonomous waterless robots for solar panel cleaning (61 units)
- Limited outdoor street cleaning robot deployments so far
- NEOM's design calls for fully autonomous city services, creating opportunity for early entrants
- Municipal tenders increasingly bundle collection with **technology integration requirements**

#### Procurement Process

- Government procurement via Etimad (national procurement portal)
- **NEOM** has its own procurement process, often seeking international partners directly
- **Local partner/agent required** for government contracts
- Mega-projects (NEOM, The Line, Jeddah Tower) have dedicated procurement teams
- **Saudization requirements:** Companies must employ minimum percentages of Saudi nationals

#### Regulatory Environment for Autonomous Robots

- **Developing:** No comprehensive autonomous robot regulations yet
- NEOM operates under special regulatory zones with more flexibility
- General trend toward tech-friendly regulation under Vision 2030
- **Saudi Standards, Metrology and Quality Organization (SASO)** certification required for imports
- Regulatory approvals may be slower than in UAE or Singapore

#### Smart City / Innovation Programs

- **Vision 2030:** Comprehensive national transformation program
- **NEOM ($500 billion mega-project):** Purpose-built smart city
- **The Line:** Linear city within NEOM, designed for autonomous services
- **Saudi Data and AI Authority (SDAIA)**
- **King Abdulaziz City for Science and Technology (KACST)**
- **National Technology Development Program (NTDP)**

#### Import Duties and Trade Barriers

- **Standard GCC customs duty: 5% CIF**
- **No US-Saudi FTA**
- **Local agent/distributor required** for government sales
- **Saudization quotas** apply to all companies operating in KSA
- **SASO certification** required for all imported products
- VAT: 15% (introduced 2018)

#### Cultural Attitudes Toward Robots

Increasingly positive, driven by government messaging around Vision 2030 and NEOM. The younger Saudi population (70% under 35) is tech-savvy. There is strong government desire to showcase cutting-edge technology.

#### Key Entry Points

- **NEOM Technology Division** -- direct engagement
- **Royal Commission for Riyadh City**
- **Ministry of Municipal and Rural Affairs (MOMRA)**
- **Saudi Investment Recycling Company (SIRC)**
- **Local agents:** Required; partner with established technology distributors

#### Recommended Entry Strategy

NEOM represents the highest-potential but longest-timeline opportunity. For faster entry, target Riyadh through a partnership with a local technology distributor with government relationships. Apply for NTDP support. Emphasize the heat-resistance angle (critical for Saudi outdoor operations). Budget for SASO certification and Saudization compliance. Consider a regional HQ in UAE to serve both markets.

### 6. Australia

**Regulatory Friendliness:** 3/5
**Ease of Entry:** 4/5
**Estimated Market Size (waste management):** USD 3.4 billion (2024), projected USD 5.3 billion by 2033
**Key City:** Sydney

#### Government Spending on Street/Public Space Cleaning

Australia's cleaning expenditure is managed at the local council level:

- **City of Sydney 2025/26:** **AUD 86 million** on cleansing and resource recovery (waste collection, street cleansing, illegal dumping removal)
- **City of Sydney 2024/25:** Brought forward **AUD 4.7 million** for additional cleansing plant and equipment
- **Bay Street depot redevelopment:** AUD 73.5 million investment in cleansing infrastructure
- **National waste management market:** USD 3.4 billion (2024), growing to USD 5.3 billion by 2033
- Each of Australia's 500+ local councils has its own cleaning budget

#### Organization

- **Local council level:** Each council manages its own cleaning services
- **State level:** State EPAs set environmental standards
- **Federal level:** Department of Climate Change, Energy, Environment and Water sets policy
- **Contracted out:** Major cleaning contractors include Cleanaway, Veolia, SUEZ
- Clean Up Australia Day (national community event) demonstrates public engagement

#### Existing Autonomous/Robotic Cleaning Deployments

- **SoftBank Robotics Whiz:** ~20 robots deployed across NSW, Victoria, and Queensland for commercial cleaning
- **CSIRO and UTS:** Collaborating on Smart Bin Technology with AI-powered sorting
- **AI-powered smart bins** with compacting and solar power deployed in multiple cities
- **Quantum Robotics (Melbourne):** Local distributor of autonomous cleaning robots
- Limited outdoor autonomous street cleaning deployments to date

#### Procurement Process

- **Council-level procurement** through tendering (AusTender for federal, state equivalents)
- Each council has its own procurement rules and thresholds
- Open, competitive tendering is standard
- **Innovation grants** available through various state and federal programs
- English-language documentation (major advantage over Asian markets)

#### Regulatory Environment for Autonomous Robots

- **State-by-state regulation:** No unified national framework for autonomous robots
- NSW, Victoria, and Queensland have autonomous vehicle trial frameworks
- **Generally cautious** approach to outdoor autonomous systems
- Work health and safety regulations add compliance requirements
- No specific sidewalk robot legislation yet
- Local council approvals needed for public space deployments

#### Smart City / Innovation Programs

- **Smart Cities and Suburbs Program** (federal, now concluded but successor programs exist)
- **Australian Renewable Energy Agency (ARENA)** -- funds clean tech
- **State innovation programs** (e.g., NSW Smart Places, VIC LaunchVic)
- **City of Sydney Tech Startups Action Plan**

#### Import Duties and Trade Barriers

- **US-Australia Free Trade Agreement (AUSFTA, 2005):** US goods generally enter **duty-free**
- Recent (2025) US imposition of 10% "reciprocal" tariff on Australian exports to US may create diplomatic friction
- No foreign ownership restrictions for technology companies
- **Standards Australia** certification may be required for electrical/safety compliance
- **Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC)** enforces product safety

#### Cultural Attitudes Toward Robots

Moderately positive but with more skepticism than Asian markets. Australians value practical, proven solutions. Environmental sustainability messaging resonates strongly. Job displacement concerns exist but are balanced by labor shortage reality.

#### Key Entry Points

- **City of Sydney Chief Technology Officer** -- AUD 86M cleaning budget
- **City of Melbourne Smart City Office**
- **CSIRO Robotics and Autonomous Systems Group**
- **Cleanaway, Veolia** -- major cleaning contractors
- **Austrade** -- Australian Trade and Investment Commission

#### Recommended Entry Strategy

## Top 5 Asia-Pacific Cities for CleanWalker's First APAC Pilot

### Ranking Methodology

Each city scored on: (1) Regulatory readiness, (2) Funding availability, (3) Labor cost pressure, (4) Competitive landscape, (5) Ease of market entry, (6) Strategic value, (7) Trade barriers, (8) IP protection, (9) Climate for quadrupedal advantage, (10) Path to commercial scale.

---

### #1: Singapore

**Score: 47/50**

**Why Singapore is the clear #1:**

- **Most advanced regulatory framework** for autonomous outdoor robots in APAC (LTA M1/T1 licenses, CETRAN testing)
- **Proven path to deployment:** WeRide went from zero to commercial operations in <6 months
- **Direct government funding:** NEA Environmental Robotics Program, NRP SGD 60M, PSG 80% co-funding
- **Zero tariffs** under USSFTA
- **Excellent IP protection** (ranked #5 globally)
- **Compact geography** means a few units can create high visibility
- **English-speaking** (reduces localization costs)
- **Quadrupedal advantage:** Singapore's extensive park connector network, HDB void decks, and hawker center surrounds have uneven terrain poorly served by wheeled robots
- **Strategic value:** Success in Singapore creates credibility for all of APAC

**Target location:** Marina Bay / Esplanade area (where WeRide already operates -- demonstrate superiority of quadrupedal approach on terraced waterfront and park areas)

**Timeline to pilot:** 6-9 months

**Score: 41/50**

## Summary Recommendations

1. **Singapore first:** Beachhead market. Apply for NEA grants, obtain LTA licenses, partner with a licensed cleaning contractor. Target 6-9 month deployment.
